EVA Laminated Fabrics

These Fabrics are manufactured using premium grade threads or yarns and latest machines under the guidance of our skilled professionals. To ensure high strength and durability, the offered Fabrics are precisely checked on several quality parameters by our quality checkers. The offered Fabrics are extensively used for stitching table mats, jute bags, sacks and various other products. We offer these Eva Foam Bonded Laminated Fabrics in different lengths, colors and thicknesses as per the specific needs of clients.

  • Properties :

  • Excellent moisture retain properties
  • Non felting and maintains shape when stretched
  • Superior shine and legendary softness
  • Strong, durable and wrinkle resistance

Foam Lamination

We only use the highest quality foam and adhesives in our lamination procedure to ensure only the best quality Foam Laminated Fabrics. We cater to various densities and thickness requirements of our customers tailored to their end-use.

Foam Laminated fabric are used in a large variety of applications such as Footwear, Shoe Lining, Upholstery, Helmets, Bra Cups, Car & Bike Seat Covers, Laptop Bags & Toys to name a few.

Foam Laminated Fabrics are used in Sports Shoes, Ladies Belly to provide extra comfort and softness to the wearer. Most shoes use Single Side Foam Laminated Fabrics as Shoe Lining as collar lining to provide extra comfort to the feet while walking or running as an industry standard. A large variety of Fabrics like checks, stripes, printed backing Fabrics are available to fulfill our customers' requirements of such collar lining.

Innerlining Fabrics

Lining fabric refers to a group of materials inserted into various garments, from skirts and shorts to dresses, jackets and coats. Such Fabrics can be made of natural or synthetic fibers and range from sheer to opaque. The purpose of lining fabric is to make your garment more wearable, long-lasting and comfortable. They are usually lightweight and have a soft or silky texture.

  • Properties :

  • To make the garment less see-through
  • To make the inside part of the garment soft and pleasant to the touch
  • To lend a luxury note to a garment
  • To improve the structure of a garment
  • To help the garment slide on easily
  • To conceal seams, padding, interfacing, etc.
