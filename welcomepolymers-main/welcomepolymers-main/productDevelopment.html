Product Development

PUR Lamination

PUR (Poly Urethane Reactive) Hot Melt is an adhesive that will bond a great many dissimilar materials with a strong and water proof glue line. Materials could be any wood compositee board, most plastic sheets, and many other materials, including thin ferrous and non-ferrous metal sheets.

PUR will contact the moisture in the air, and allow it to form stronger bonds after the curing process is completed. PUR has a highly flexible and strong adhesion, and suitable for a wide range of climates. With that extraordinary feature, PUR adhesive has become standard adhesive for hard-to-find materials

PUL Fabrics for Use

A PU laminated fabric is one in which a thin film of polyurethane is laminated on one or both sides by a cloth. Whereas a PU coated fabric is fabric (like polyester or nylon) that has a waterproof PU coating on one side.

PU laminate cloth is waterproof, breathes and stretches somewhat, and is soft and flexible. It can usually be machine-washed and dried, and cleaned with dilute bleach or alcohol. Most PUL fabric is made by laminating lightweight polyester interlock knit fabric to a 1 mil thick film of polyurethane. There are two processes used for lamination: solvent lamination, which fuses the fabric and polyurethane film into a single monolithic fabric, and hot melt, which uses heat-activated glue to adhere the Fabrics together
